The Energy-Water Relation in Data Center Warehouses

As highlighted by the International Energy Agency (IEA, 2025) and Li et al. (2023), the environmental impact of large-scale AI computation is a dual-resource crisis where electricity and water consumption are both affected. Traditional server architectures generate high heat during workload, requiring continuous cooling mechanisms to maintain operational (Patel et al., 2002). This creates a dependency on local water networks for water cooling systems.

According to recent research estimates (Siddik et al.; Guidi et al., 2024), more than half of the water utilized directly on-site at these facilities is potable drinking water. Unlike energy grids, which can slowly transition toward renewable solar or wind sources (Ewim et al., 2023), freshwater consumption is just a depletion of reservoirs (Gorey, 2026).
