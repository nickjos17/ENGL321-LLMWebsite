Environmental Impacts of LLM's and Data Centers

By Nicholas K. & Jackson N.

As cryptocurrency and artificial intelligence occupy more of the global economic space, their underlying processing infrastructure—warehouse-scale data centers—is rapidly expanding. This digital boom introduces significant, localized resource stress across electricity grids, clean water reserves, air quality indices, and acoustic environments.

Grid Demands & Power Scaling

Energy Infrastructure

U.S. data centers consumed an estimated 176 terawatt-hours (TWh) of electricity in 2023, making up roughly 4.4% of total nationwide power. Driven by intensive LLM training clusters, this industrial energy consumption footprint is projected to double or triple by 2028.

Cooling Systems & Water Depletion

Resource Allocation

Warehouse-scale servers generate intense heat, relying heavily on Water Cooling Systems. Google’s data centers in The Dalles consumed 355 million gallons of water in 2021—accounting for 29% of the city's entire water supply. Unfortunately for us, over 50% of server cooling water is gathered directly from clean, potable drinking water sources.

Carbon Tracking & Grid Emissions

Atmospheric Impact

In 2018, domestic data infrastructure emitted 31.5 million tons of greenhouse gases. Driven by a surging grid demand heavily reliant on fossil fuels and coal plants, annual data center carbon output has skyrocketed to an estimated 105 million tons of CO2.

Acoustic Pollution & Hearing Risks

Local Community Safety

Massive industrial HVAC cooling arrays and emergency backup diesel generators produce continuous noise pollution. Exterior ambient sound levels near residential boundaries hover between 55 to 85 dBA, while interior facility noise peaks up to 96 dBA. A threshold capable of causing permanent hearing damage.
