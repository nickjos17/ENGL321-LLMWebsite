Atmospheric Emissions and the Localized Acoustic Burden

The physical footprint of LLM's infrastructure introduces harsh environmental challenges, altering both atmospheric condition and local soundscapes. As evaluated by Guidi et al. (2024), the power requirements of data warehouses rely heavily on regional electrical grids that still burn carbon-intensive fossil fuels. This accounts for a staggering annual emission baseline of over 105 million tons of CO2. This rapid rise actively drives up atmospheric greenhouse gases while concentrating air pollution around the generating utility plants.

The physical operation of warehouse-scale facilities introduces a persistent acoustic burden on surrounding communities. High cooling demands require industrial HVAC chiller fans and heavy backup diesel generators that generate uninterrupted, low-frequency noise pollution (Richardson, 2024). According to human health impact frameworks established by Yu and Gao (2025), prolonged exposure to these decibel levels disrupts native wildlife migration patterns and induces severe sleep degradation and cardiovascular stress in nearby residents.
